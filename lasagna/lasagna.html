Lasagna

About Lasagna

Originating from Italy, this dish is a form of pasta famous for its scrumptious taste and it's accessibilty to cook for people of most skillsets. When referring to Lasagna, people either think of the type of noodle or the the dish of lasagna. The dish is made up of different sheets with meats, cheese and other toppings. There are different options for vegans and vegetarians nowadays as well, alongside the traditonal meatier options.

Expanding from the origins of lasagna, the word was used to describe a cooking pot, in which food was prepared in, rather than the dish itself. They are dated back to the early 13th century in Italy, meaning they are a classic food.

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • 500-750g of Mince Beef
  • 90g of Prosciutto
  • Tomato Sauce
  • 200ml Hot Beef Stock
  • Grated nutmeg
  • 300g of fresh Lasagne
  • White Sauce
  • 125g ball Mozzarella

Steps

  1. Heat two tablespoons of olive oil and cook 750g beaf mince in two batches for aout 10 minutes.
  2. Slice 4 pieces of proscuiutto
  3. Pour 800g passata and 200 ml hot beef stock. Add some grated nutmeg and then season.
  4. Bring it up to the cooker and simmer for 30 minutes until the sauce looks rich.
  5. Heat oven to 180 degrees celsius and lightly oil to a ovenproof dish.
  6. Spoon one third of the meat sauce into the dish, then cover the sauce with the fresh lasagna sheets.
  7. Repeat previous steps until you have three layers of pasta. Cove the remaining with 390g of white sauce.
  8. Scatter 125g torn Mozzarella over the top.
  9. Arrange the rest of the prosciutto to sit on top of the dish. Bake for 45 minutes more until the top is bubbling and browned.
